Resume Tips & Best Practices

  • Keep your resume concise and focused on the most relevant information.
  • Use action verbs to describe your experiences and achievements.
  • Tailor your resume for each job application.
  • Highlight your skills that match the job requirements.
  • Use a clean, professional format that is easy to read.
  • Proofread carefully to avoid spelling and grammar mistakes.

  • Include both technical and soft skills relevant to the position.
  • List skills in order of relevance to the job.
  • Be specific about your level of proficiency with each skill.
  • Include industry-specific terminology and keywords.

  • List your work experience in reverse chronological order.
  • Include your job title, company name, location, and dates of employment.
  • Use bullet points to describe your responsibilities and achievements.
  • Quantify your achievements whenever possible (e.g., "increased sales by 20%").

  • Include your degree, major, university name, and graduation date.
  • List any relevant coursework, projects, or academic achievements.
  • Include certifications and professional development courses.

  • Use consistent formatting throughout your resume.
  • Choose a professional font and appropriate font size (10-12pt).
  • Use white space effectively to make your resume easy to scan.
  • Save your resume as a PDF to preserve formatting across devices.
